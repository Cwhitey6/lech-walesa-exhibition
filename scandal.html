Collaboration Scandal

Accusations and Archives

Lech Wałęsa was accused in the 1970s of working with the communists as an informant under the codename “Bolek,” relaying information about Poland, though he denied all allegations and the original charges were dropped in 2000. The scandal resurfaced in 2016 when the widow of General Czesław Kiszczak attempted to sell documents to the Polish government that were said to contain hundreds of notes written by “Bolek,” including reports on events in Poland and meetings with secret police members, dating from 1970–1976, before Wałęsa formed Solidarity and became president; the papers were seized that same night. In later years Wałęsa admitted he agreed to certain circumstances but claimed he never intended to follow through, yet he was charged in 2021 with lying under oath about signing the documents, and in 2023 the National Institute of Remembrance announced a trial for his false 2016 testimony, which remains ongoing as of 2025. He also faced separate accusations of hate speech in 2013 for remarks concerning the gay rights movement.

Archival files, expert reports, and court rulings all form part of the contested record around these accusations.
