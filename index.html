Who Was Lech Wałęsa?

Throughout his life, Lech Wałęsa wore many different hats. From being a sibling in a household of 9, to serving in the army as a corporal, to becoming an electrician working at a shipyard, Lech did many things. However, his most significant role in life was as the national chairman of the Polish Solidarity movement and eventually, as the first President of Poland. Despite being jailed numerous times for speaking out against the communist government, he persevered to help secure free elections for Poland and became a central figure in forming the first non-communist government in the Eastern Bloc. Presently, he tours around the world giving talks as an advocate for international and interpersonal solidarity, and is an associate assisting the Lech Wałęsa Institute in its goal to protect and promote the principles of democracy for the next generation by educating the youth, protecting the environment, and spreading the history of Polish politics for all the world to hear.
